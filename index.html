Answer to the question no-01(a)

Janapadas

The state as undivided Bebgal was not born at a time. It flourished through Janapadas. From different inscriptions and literary works, we came to know about sixteen Janapadas of Bengal. Some of them discuss bellow:

Gaur: Though the name of Gaur' seems familiar, there are no clear ravidences about which area was referred to as Gaur in ancient time. In the 7th century, Sasanka was called the Gaurraj (King of Gaur). Its capital was Karnasubarna. Gaur was situated at the present Murshidabad.

Banga: Bengal was a very ancient Janapada. A Janapada named Banga was built to the east and the south-east of present Bangladesh. It is surmised that a nation named 'Banga' lived here.

Pundra: Pundra was one of the most important Janapada of ancient Bengal. It is said tht a nation called Pundra built up thid Janapada. Pundra of that time was extended at least across the regions of Bogura, Rangpur, Dinajpur and Rajshahi. Pundranagar was the name of the capital of the Pundras.

Harikela: The writers of the seventh century have also discribed another region called Harikela. Harikela was situated at the end of eastern Bengal. It can be assumed that Harikela spread from Sylhet to Chittagong at the present time.

Samatata: Samatata was situated in the east and south-east Bengal as a neighboring janapada of Banga. Some think that Samatata was the ancient name of present Cumilla. In the seventh century its capital was the place name Bara Kamta, 12 miles west of Cumilla city.

Barendra: Another Janapada of ancient Bengal could be known in the name of Barendree, Barendra or Barendra Bhumi. It was also a janapada of North Bengal. It is thought that Barendra was located in the vast area of Pundra. The Barendra region was ectended across a vast area of Bogura, Dinajpur and Rajshahi region as well as probably of Pabna region.

We get a fair conception of geographical shape, demarcation, political characteristics of the then Bengal from the Janapada of ancient Bengal. Then there was no political unity in ancient Bengal.

Answer to the question no-04(b)

Six Points

To get rid of the colonial rule and exploitation of the Pakistan, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman announced the six points program in 1966. The purpose of Bangabandhu was to free East Pakistan from the dsicrimination through realising Six Points semand. Basically after the end of the war between India and Pakistan, Bnagabandhu raised voice against the extreme negligence of the West Pakistan. Government to the security of East Pakistan. and limitless disparty toward East Pakistan Opposition leaders convened a conference on 5-6 February in 1966 in Lahore. Bangabandhu reached Lahore along with the tio Awami League leaders. In the conference Bangabandhu presented the 'Six Points' demand which was rejected by the leaders of the conference.Bangabandhu walked out of the conference and returned to Dhaka disclosing Six Points to the press. On 21st February a pamphlet titled 'Amader Bachar Dabi: Choy Dafa Karmashuchi' (Our Demand for Survival: Six Points Program) was published in the name of Bangabandhu. The Six Points were as follows:

  1. The constitution should provide for a Federation of Pakistan in its true sense on the basis of the Lahore Resolution and the Parliamentary form of government with supremacy of a Legislature directly elected on the basis of universal adult franchise.
  2. The federal government should deal with only two subjects: Defence and Foregin Affairs. All other residuary subjects shall be vested in the federal states.
  3. Two separate, but freely convertible currencies for two woings should be introduce; or if this is not feasible, there should be one currency for the whole country.But effective constitution provisitions should be introduce to stop the flight of capital from East to West pakistan.
  4. The power of taxation and revenue collection shall be vested in the federating units. The federation will be entitled to deposit a certain portion in the fedearal fund. The federal government shall use this fund to meet its expenditures.
  5. The federal units shall have the full authority to regulate foregin exchenge earnings and trade links. The foregin exchange requirements of the federal government should be met by the two wings equally or in a ratio to be fixed.
  6. To safeguard regional solidarity and national security the provinces should have the suthority to form and control their own militiaor paramilitary force.

The six points of Bangabandhu was accepted in the Working Committee meeting of the Awami League held on 13 March 1966. Then Bangabandhu campaigned for creating public opinion in favor of the Six Points. He went to different places and delivered apeech on this . He termed these Six Points as our demand for survival. As a result of Bangabandhu's campaign, Six Points gained public support very rapidly. Being panicked the Government of Ayub Khan started arresting the leaders and activities of the Awami League. During this time Ayub Khan visited East Pakistan and delivered speeches in various public meetings where he termed Six Points as seditious asd a threat of the unity. The government of Ayub Khan arrested Bangabandhu on 9 May in 1966 as Six Points gained popularity day by day. On 7th June a general strike was observed to protrest the arrest of Bangabandhu. During hartal a number of individuals were killed in police firing. In protest of this killing opposition stage walk out from the Provincial Assembly on 8 June. In 1968 the Government put Bangabandhu on trial accusing him in the historic Agartala Case. The case led to mass upsurge in 1969 and the government was forced to release Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. The key factor in Awami league's election manifasto in 1970 was this Six Points program. The Awami League sought public mandate in favor of the Six Points program in the general election of 1970. A landside victory was gained with the absolute mandate from the people of East Pakistan in favor of Six Points program. Yet, this Six Points program could not be implemented. The Six Points program ended with the commencement of the War of Liberation that followed the genocide caused by the Pakistan occupational force. After Wards Bangladesh gained independence after nine months Liberation war.

Human Physiology and Behavior

Human Physiology: It is a brance of behavior that deals with the function and activities of living matter and the physical and chemicla processes and mechanisms involved.

It is the science of the mechanical, physical and biological functions of normal humands or human tisssues or organs. For example, endocrine and nervous systems of human body perform major role in the reception and transmission of signals that integrate function in animals.

The study of physiology occurs at many levels, including organisms, oragans, organ systems, cells, and bimolecules. The principal level of focus of physiology is at the level of organs and systems.

Anatomy: It is the study of the form and structure and relationships among the various body parts. Physiology is closely related to anatomy and they are intrinsically linked.

Anatomy Physiology
It is the study of the physical structure and apearance of the body. It is the study of the internal functions of the body.
It deals with the structure of the parts of an organism. It deals with the way taht the parts function together.

Homeostasis: It means maintenance of static or constant conditions in the enternal environment of teh body. all the tissues of the body help to maintain these constant conditions. Examples:

  • Lung provides O2 to the cell & eliminates CO2 and other volatile metabolic end products from cell.
  • Kidney maintains constant ionic concentration.
  • GIT always provide nutrition to the cell.


Behavior: It is the range of action and mannerisms made by individuals, organisms, systems or artificial entities in conjubction with themselves or their environment, which includes the other organs or systems around as well as the physical environment. It is the respons of the syste or organism to various stimuli or inputs, whether internal or external, consious or subconsnious and voluntary or invluntary.

Human Behavior: It refers to the full range of physical and emotional behaviors that humans angage in biologically, intellectually etc. and are influenced by culrure, attitudes, emotions, values, ethics, authority, rapport, and genetics.

It is capacity of mental,physical, emotional and social activities experienced during the five stages of a human being's life- prenatal, infency, childhood, adolesence and adulthood.

Factors affecting human behavior:

  1. Genetics: Charles Darwin in his book On the Origin of Species proposed that, the pattern of behavior are somehow influenced by inheritance from parents.
  2. Social Norms: Social norms, the unspoken group of rules, shape not just our behavior but also our attitude. An individual's behavior varies depending on the groups they are a part of. Without social norms, human society wolud not function.
  3. Creativity: Creativity is assumed to be present within every individual. Creativity pushes people past their comfort zone. Creatrivity has kept people alive during harsh condition and it has also made certain individuals wealthy. We use creativity in our daily lives as well.
  4. Core faith/ beliefs: Core faith can be manifasted in the froms of religion, philosophy, culture and personal belief and often affects the way a person can behave
  5. Religion: Religious belief plays a large importance in society to have a effect on human behavior.
  6. Morals and Emotions: Morals are another factor of core faith that affects the way a person behaves. Emotoins connected to morals including shame, pride and discomfort and these can change the way a person acts.
  7. Culture: The beliefs of certain cultures are taught to children from such a young age that they are greatly affected as they grow up.
  8. Attitude: It is an espression of favor or disfavor toward a person, place, thing or event. The attitude of human beings alters among each individual. Everyone has a different attitude towards different things
  9. Motivation: It may influence a person to behave positively towards a negative situation or vice versa.

Basic Structure of Human Body:
Part-1 :: Nervous System

Defination: Nervous system is a network od specialized cell that communicate information about an organism surrounding and itself. It passes this information and causes the reaction in other parts of the body.

It is composed of neurons and glial cell and that cells aided the function of the neuron. The neurons of the nervous system of animals are interconnected in complex arrangement and use electrochemical signals and neurotransmiters to trandmit impulse from one neuron to the next.

The human nervous system contains approximately 10 billion nerve cells/neurons. The neurons are the basic bulding blocks of the nervious system.

The peripheral part of the nervous system is so called because it is outside of the CNS. Neuron: A nerve cell with all its processes is called neuron. Neuron is the structural and functional unit of nervous system. It contains of cell body or soma and two types of processes- Axon and Dendrite.

Different part of neuron:

  1. Axon: It is the process of a nerve cell body that carries impulse away from it. Nerve fiver which carry impulse to the CNS are called afferent and those carry impulse from the CNS to the periphery are known as efferent. It is generally long with few branches.

    Axon may be covered with an insulating layer called myelin sheath, which is formed by schwann cells. The myelin sheath is not continuos but divided into sections, aeparated at a regular intervals by the nodes of Ranvier.

  2. Dendrite: It is the process that collects impulse from other neurons and carries them towards the cell body. It is generally short with many branches and the number of branches varies from zero to numerous.

  3. Cell body / Soma: It is enlarged portion of a neuron that most closely resembled other cells. It coordinates the metabolic activity of the neuron.

Synapse: Synapse is the junction region where one neuron ends and the other begins. In a synapse, the terminal branches of the axon(presynaptic cell) of a neuron come in contact with the cell body or the dendrites of another(postsynaptic cell).

Functoinal anatomy of synapse: Before synapsing with a cell body or dendrites, the axon terminal of the presynaptic neuron is called synaptic knob or terminal knob or buton. The membrane of the synaptic knob is calles presynaptic membrane and that of the cell body or dendrites of the post synaptic neuron is called postsynaptic membrane. Both these membrane are separeted by synaptic cleft having a width of 200-300 angstroms. The presynaptic knob contains numerous mitocondria and vesicles which contain neurotransmitter substances. The mitocondria supplies energy that is needed for the synthesis of the neurotransmitter substances.
